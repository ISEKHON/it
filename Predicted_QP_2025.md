## SECTION A ANSWERS (Any 6 × 2.5 marks = 15 marks)

### 1(a) What is the difference between Hardware and Software? (2.5 marks)

**Answer:**

| Hardware | Software |
|----------|----------|
| Physical components you can touch | Programs and instructions (intangible) |
| Examples: Monitor, keyboard, CPU, mouse | Examples: Windows, MS Office, games |
| Damaged by physical impact | Corrupted by viruses, bugs |
| Cannot work without software | Cannot run without hardware |
| One-time purchase | May require updates/subscriptions |

**Hardware** provides the physical structure, while **software** provides instructions to make hardware functional. Both are essential for a computer system to operate.

---

### 1(b) Define ROM and its types. (2.5 marks)

**Answer:**

**ROM (Read-Only Memory):** Non-volatile memory that retains data permanently even without power. It stores firmware and boot instructions.

**Types:**
1. **PROM:** Programmable once by user
2. **EPROM:** Erasable by UV light, reprogrammable
3. **EEPROM:** Electrically erasable, byte-level modification
4. **Flash Memory:** Block-level erase, used in USB drives and SSDs

**Use:** BIOS, firmware, embedded systems

---

### 1(c) What are the advantages of SSD over HDD? (2.5 marks)

**Answer:**

**Advantages of SSD:**
1. **Speed:** 10-100x faster read/write (3,500 MB/s vs 150 MB/s)
2. **Durability:** No moving parts, shock-resistant
3. **Silent:** No noise (HDD has spinning/clicking sounds)
4. **Power Efficient:** Lower power consumption, better for laptops
5. **Lightweight:** Compact and portable
6. **Instant Access:** No spin-up time required

**Disadvantage:** More expensive per GB than HDD

---

### 1(d) Write a short note on Touch Screen as an input device. (2.5 marks)

**Answer:**

**Touch Screen** is both an input and output device that allows direct interaction with display using fingers or stylus.

**Technology:**
- **Resistive:** Pressure-sensitive (cheaper, less accurate)
- **Capacitive:** Electrical charge detection (smartphones, tablets)

**Gestures:** Tap, swipe, pinch-to-zoom, long press

**Uses:** Smartphones, tablets, ATMs, kiosks, POS systems

**Advantages:** Intuitive, no external device needed, fast input  
**Disadvantages:** Screen gets dirty, less precise than mouse

### 1(e) What is the role of Headers and Footers in word processing? (2.5 marks)

**Answer:**

**Header:** Top margin area that appears on every page (or selected pages)  
**Footer:** Bottom margin area that appears on every page

**Uses:**
- **Page numbers:** "Page 1 of 10" in footer
- **Document title:** Chapter name in header
- **Date/Author:** Document info in header
- **Company logo:** Branding in header
- **Copyright notice:** Legal info in footer

**Options:**
- Different first page (title page without header)

**Insert:** Insert → Header/Footer → Choose style

### 1(f) Explain MICR technology and its applications. (2.5 marks)

**Answer:**

**MICR (Magnetic Ink Character Recognition):** Technology that reads characters printed with magnetic ink.

**Working:**
1. Characters printed in special magnetic ink
2. MICR reader magnetizes ink
3. Detects magnetic pattern
4. Converts to digital data

**Characters:** E-13B font (numbers 0-9 and special symbols)

**Applications:**
- **Cheque processing:** Banks use for fast, secure cheque clearing
- Reads cheque number, account number, bank code
- Processes thousands of cheques per hour

**Advantages:** Very secure (hard to forge), high accuracy, works even if overwritten

### 1(h) Differentiate between CD and DVD. (2.5 marks)

**Answer:**

| Feature | CD | DVD |
|---------|-----|-----|
| **Full Form** | Compact Disc | Digital Versatile Disc |
| **Capacity** | 700 MB (max) | 4.7 GB - 17 GB |
| **Laser** | Red (780 nm) | Red (650 nm - shorter wavelength) |
| **Pit Size** | Larger | Smaller (higher density) |
| **Use** | Music, small data | Movies, large data |
| **Video Quality** | None (audio only) | SD (480p), some HD |
| **Release Year** | 1982 | 1995 |

**Both use optical technology (pits and lands), but DVD has higher data density.**

### 2. Explain the five generations of computers with their key features, examples, and technological advancements. (15 marks)

**Answer:**

## FIRST GENERATION (1940-1956): Vacuum Tubes (3 marks)

**Technology:**
- **Vacuum tubes** for circuitry (thousands per computer)
- **Magnetic drums** for memory
- **Punch cards** for input

**Characteristics:**
- **Size:** Room-sized, extremely large
- **Speed:** Very slow (milliseconds)
- **Heat:** Generated enormous heat
- **Power:** Consumed huge amounts of electricity
- **Reliability:** Frequent failures (tubes burned out)
- **Cost:** Extremely expensive (millions of dollars)

**Programming:** Machine language (binary 0s and 1s only)

**Examples:**
- **ENIAC (1946):** First general-purpose electronic computer
- **UNIVAC (1951):** First commercial computer
- **EDVAC:** Stored program concept

**Limitations:**
- Not portable
- Required air-conditioned rooms
- Limited programming capability
- High maintenance

---

## SECOND GENERATION (1956-1963): Transistors (3 marks)

**Technology:**
- **Transistors** replaced vacuum tubes
- **Magnetic core memory**
- **Magnetic tapes** for storage

**Characteristics:**
- **Size:** Smaller than first generation
- **Speed:** Faster (microseconds)
- **Heat:** Less heat generation
- **Reliability:** More reliable than vacuum tubes
- **Power:** Lower power consumption
- **Cost:** Less expensive

**Programming:** 
- **Assembly language** introduced (mnemonics instead of binary)
- **High-level languages** emerged: COBOL, FORTRAN

**Examples:**
- **IBM 1401:** Popular business computer
- **IBM 7094:** Scientific computing
- **CDC 1604**

**Advantages:**
- Smaller size
- More reliable
- Faster processing
- Less maintenance

---

## THIRD GENERATION (1964-1971): Integrated Circuits (3 marks)

**Technology:**
- **ICs (Integrated Circuits):** Thousands of transistors on single silicon chip
- **Semiconductor memory**
- **Keyboards and monitors** introduced

**Characteristics:**
- **Size:** Much smaller (mini computers)
- **Speed:** Very fast (nanoseconds)
- **Reliability:** Highly reliable
- **Cost:** Affordable for businesses
- **Portability:** More portable

**Operating System:** 
- **Multiprogramming:** Run multiple programs
- **Time-sharing:** Multiple users simultaneously

**Programming:** High-level languages became standard (BASIC, Pascal, C)

**Examples:**
- **IBM 360 series:** Mainframe computers
- **PDP-8:** First successful minicomputer
- **PDP-11**

**Advantages:**
- Portable
- Efficient
- Reliable
- Compatible software

---

## FOURTH GENERATION (1971-Present): Microprocessors (3 marks)

**Technology:**
- **Microprocessor:** Entire CPU on single chip
- **LSI/VLSI:** Large Scale/Very Large Scale Integration
- **Semiconductor memory** (RAM, ROM chips)

**Characteristics:**
- **Size:** Desktop PCs, laptops, mobile devices
- **Speed:** Extremely fast (picoseconds)
- **Cost:** Very affordable
- **Personal use:** Available to general public

**Key Developments:**
- **1971:** Intel 4004 - first microprocessor
- **1981:** IBM PC - personal computer revolution
- **1984:** Apple Macintosh - GUI popularized
- **1990s:** Internet era, multimedia PCs
- **2000s:** Laptops, mobile computing

**Features:**
- Graphical User Interface (GUI)
- Networking capability
- Internet connectivity
- Multimedia support

**Examples:**
- **Intel processors:** 8086, Pentium, Core i3/i5/i7/i9
- **Computers:** IBM PC, Apple Macintosh, modern PCs and laptops

**Programming:** Object-oriented (C++, Java, Python), web technologies

---

## FIFTH GENERATION (Present-Future): Artificial Intelligence (3 marks)

**Technology:**
- **ULSI:** Ultra Large Scale Integration

**Focus:**

**Technologies:**

### DETAILED EXPLANATION:

## 1. SCANNER (6 marks)

**Definition:**
An input device that converts physical documents, photos, and images into digital format using light sensors.

**Working Principle:**
1. **Light Source:** Bright light (LED/fluorescent) illuminates document
2. **Reflection:** Light reflects off document surface
3. **Mirror System:** Mirrors direct reflected light to sensor
4. **CCD/CIS Sensor:** 
   - **CCD (Charge-Coupled Device):** High quality, expensive
   - **CIS (Contact Image Sensor):** Compact, cheaper
5. **Analog to Digital:** Sensor converts light intensity to digital values
6. **Image Formation:** Software processes data into image file

**Types of Scanners:**

**a) Flatbed Scanner (Most Common):**
- Document placed face-down on glass plate
- Scanning head moves underneath
- High quality (600-4800 DPI)
- Can scan books, magazines, photos

**b) Sheet-fed Scanner:**
- Document fed through like a fax machine
- Compact and fast
- Cannot scan books (only loose sheets)

**c) Handheld Scanner:**
- Manual swipe across document
- Portable but less accurate
- Used for receipts, small documents

**d) Drum Scanner:**
- Professional high-resolution scanning
- Used in printing industry
- Very expensive

```
│  │    Moving Scan Head       │ │ ← Moves left to right
│  │  [Light][Mirrors][Sensor] │ │
│  └───────┬───────────────────┘ │
│          ↓ Light                │
│  ════════════════════════       │ ← Document
│          ↑ Reflected light      │

           ↓

      (Digital Image)
```

**Scanning Process:**
1. Place document on glass plate
2. Close lid (for better quality)
3. Open scanning software
4. Set preferences:
   - Resolution (DPI): 150 (draft), 300 (standard), 600+ (high quality)
   - Color mode: Color, Grayscale, Black & White
   - File format: JPEG, PNG, PDF, TIFF
5. Preview scan (optional)
6. Click "Scan" button
7. Image saved to computer

**Resolution (DPI - Dots Per Inch):**
- **150 DPI:** Draft quality, small file size
- **300 DPI:** Standard (photos, documents)
- **600 DPI:** High quality (professional photos)
- **1200+ DPI:** Professional printing

**Applications:**
1. **Office:**
   - Digitize paper documents
   - Create searchable PDFs
   - Archive old records

2. **Photography:**
   - Scan old photos to preserve
   - Create digital photo albums

3. **Publishing:**
   - Scan artwork for printing
   - Magazine/book production

4. **Healthcare:**
   - Scan medical records, X-rays
   - Patient documentation

5. **Education:**
   - Scan notes, assignments
   - Create digital libraries

**Advantages:**
✅ Converts physical to digital (paperless office)  
✅ OCR technology extracts text from scanned documents  
✅ Preserves old photos and documents  
✅ Easy sharing (email scanned files)  
✅ Multiple copies without degradation  

**Disadvantages:**
❌ Slow for large volumes (1-2 pages per minute)  
❌ Quality depends on scanner resolution  
❌ Requires computer and software  
❌ Large file sizes for high-resolution scans

## 2. MICROPHONE (6 marks)

**Definition:**
An input device that captures sound waves and converts them into electrical signals, which are then digitized for computer processing.

**Working Principle:**

**Sound → Mechanical Vibration → Electrical Signal → Digital Data**

1. **Sound Waves:** Pressure variations in air
2. **Diaphragm:** Thin membrane vibrates when sound waves hit
3. **Transducer:** Converts vibration to electrical signal
4. **Amplification:** Signal amplified (often very weak)
5. **ADC (Analog-to-Digital Converter):** Converts to digital data
6. **Computer Storage:** Digital audio file created

**Types of Microphones:**

**a) Dynamic Microphone:**
- **Principle:** Electromagnetic induction (moving coil in magnetic field)
- **Durability:** Rugged, can handle loud sounds
- **Power:** No external power needed
- **Use:** Live performances, stage, podcasts
- **Examples:** Shure SM58 (industry standard)

**b) Condenser Microphone:**
- **Principle:** Capacitance change (charged plates)
- **Quality:** High sensitivity, better sound quality
- **Power:** Needs external power (phantom power or battery)
- **Use:** Studio recording, professional audio
- **Examples:** Blue Yeti, Audio-Technica AT2020

**c) Ribbon Microphone:**
- **Principle:** Thin metal ribbon vibrates in magnetic field
- **Quality:** Warm, natural sound
- **Fragile:** Delicate, expensive
- **Use:** High-end studio recording

**d) USB Microphone:**
- **Connection:** Direct USB connection (no audio interface needed)
- **Convenience:** Plug-and-play
- **Use:** Podcasting, gaming, home recording
- **Examples:** Blue Yeti, Samson Meteor

**Polar Patterns (Directionality):**

**a) Cardioid (Heart-shaped):**
- Picks up sound from front
- Rejects sound from back and sides
- Most common pattern
- Use: Solo vocals, instruments

**b) Omnidirectional:**
- Picks up sound equally from all directions (360°)
- Use: Room ambience, conference calls

**c) Bidirectional (Figure-8):**
- Picks up from front and back
- Rejects from sides
- Use: Two-person interviews

**d) Shotgun (Super-cardioid):**
- Very narrow pickup (front only)
- Use: Film, video production

**Microphone Specifications:**

**1. Frequency Response:**
- Human hearing: 20 Hz to 20,000 Hz
- Voice: 80 Hz to 12,000 Hz
- Flat response: Accurate reproduction

**2. Sensitivity:**
- How much output for given sound level
- Measured in dB (decibels)
- Higher = picks up quieter sounds

**3. Impedance:**
- Low (150-600 Ω): Professional use
- High (10,000+ Ω): Consumer use

**Applications:**

**1. Communication:**
- Voice calls (Skype, Zoom)
- Video conferencing
- Voice chat (gaming, Discord)

**2. Content Creation:**
- Podcasting
- YouTube videos
- Streaming (Twitch)
- Music recording

**3. Speech Recognition:**
- Voice assistants (Siri, Alexa)
- Dictation software
- Voice typing (Google Docs)

**4. Entertainment:**
- Karaoke
- Live performances
- Gaming communication

**5. Professional:**
- Radio broadcasting
- Film/TV production
- Voiceovers and dubbing
- Phone systems

**Connection Types:**
- **XLR:** Professional (balanced, low noise)
- **3.5mm jack:** Consumer devices (phones, laptops)
- **USB:** Direct digital connection
- **Wireless:** Bluetooth, RF (Radio Frequency)

**Advantages:**
✅ Essential for voice communication  
✅ Hands-free operation (voice commands)  
✅ Natural input method (speaking)  
✅ Accessibility (for visually impaired)  
✅ Faster than typing (120 words/min speech vs 40 typing)  

**Disadvantages:**
❌ Background noise interference  
❌ Requires quiet environment for quality  
❌ Accent/language recognition issues  
❌ Privacy concerns (always listening)  
❌ Not suitable for confidential environments  

**Best Practices:**
- Position 6-12 inches from mouth
- Use pop filter (reduces plosives: P, B, T sounds)
- Quiet environment for best quality
- Adjust gain (input level) properly
- Test before important recordings
